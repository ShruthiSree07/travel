# Marketing — WanderFive

## Positioning statement
For the casual trip planner staring at 40 open tabs, WanderFive is the instant-shortlist search that turns any place name into 5 places worth visiting — unlike scrolling through TripAdvisor or a generic map full of unranked pins.

## Target persona
- **Name/archetype:** Priya, about to land somewhere new for a weekend and wants a plan before she lands.
- **Daily job-to-be-done:** Get a fast, trustworthy shortlist of what to see in an unfamiliar place.
- **Current workaround:** Opens TripAdvisor, a travel blog, and Google Maps in three tabs and cross-references.
- **Pain in the workaround:** 15-20 minutes of skimming ad-heavy lists to end up with the same 5 places she could've gotten in one search.
- **Decision trigger:** Any moment she's about to go somewhere new — a trip, a layover, a weekend drive.

## Brand voice
- **Fast** — No onboarding, no clutter. Type, get an answer. "5 places, one search" not "your personalized travel companion."
- **Trustworthy-but-honest** — Never pretends to know more than it does; clearly flags when it's guessing.
- **Warm** — Travel should feel inviting, not like filling out a form. Copy stays human, never corporate.

## Tagline
Variants:
1. "Type a place. Get 5 worth visiting." *(primary)*
2. "Skip the tabs. Get the shortlist."
3. "Where to next, in one search."

## Anti-positioning
- Not a trip-planning suite (no itineraries, bookings, or maps) — it's a shortlist generator, nothing more.
- Not a live data source — it won't tell you today's hours or ticket prices.
- Not a social/reviews platform — no user accounts, ratings, or comments.

## Landing page copy

**H1:** Type a place. Get 5 worth visiting.
**H2:** No tabs, no accounts, no scrolling — just a fast, honest shortlist.
**Benefits:**
- Skip the 20-minute research spiral
- Get 5 places, not 200 pins
- Works for any place, anywhere

**Primary CTA:** Try It Now
**Trust line:** No sign-up. No cost. No data stored.

### Feature blurbs
**Instant Shortlist**
Type any city, town, or landmark and get exactly 5 places worth seeing — no scrolling through review pages to find them.

**Honest About Its Limits**
When a location is obscure or made up, WanderFive says so and gives you general pointers instead of pretending to know a place it doesn't.

**Zero Setup**
No account, no API key, nothing to configure — open it and search.

## FAQ (selected)
**Q: What does WanderFive actually do?**
You type a location, it returns 5 places worth visiting there, each with a short description — no maps, no bookings, no reviews.

**Q: Who is this NOT for?**
Anyone who needs live hours, prices, or bookable itineraries — this is a shortlist tool, not a trip-planning suite.

**Q: How is this different from just Googling it?**
It skips the ad-heavy list pages and gives you exactly 5 curated picks in one search instead of 200 unranked results.

**Q: Does it store my searches?**
No — WanderFive is fully stateless. Nothing about your search is saved anywhere.
